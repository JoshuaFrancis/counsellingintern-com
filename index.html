Platform overview

Three tools that connect training sites to the next generation of clinicians.

01

Practicum placement matching

A searchable database of accredited practicum and internship sites matched to student profiles by geographic preference, clinical population interest, theoretical orientation, and programme requirements. Placement coordinators at universities use the platform to track student placement status, supervisor credentials, and hours accumulation against licensing board requirements. Sites post available positions with rotation details, client population descriptions, and supervisor qualifications, reducing the email-based coordination that currently dominates practicum administration.

02

Supervision management tools

Digital supervision logs, case presentation templates, and competency tracking tools for both supervisors and interns. Regulatory bodies including provincial psychology colleges and the Canadian Psychological Association require specific documentation of supervision hours, case types, and competency development. The platform automates this record-keeping and generates the formatted reports required for registration applications, eliminating the manual documentation burden that both supervisors and interns currently manage in spreadsheets and email threads.

03

Professional development resources

A library of continuing education modules, ethics case studies, clinical skill workshops, and career guidance resources tailored to psychology interns and early-career practitioners navigating the transition from training to independent practice. Content covers licensure pathways across Canadian provinces and US states, scope of practice decisions, private practice setup, supervision best practices, and the clinical skills most often identified as gaps in formal training programmes. Accessible on the same platform as placement coordination tools.
